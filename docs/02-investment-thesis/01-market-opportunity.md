## Chapter 2: The Academic Foundation

### 2.1 Trend Following: The 140-Year Anomaly

**The Evidence (1880-2025):**

Trend following—buying assets going up, selling assets going down—has generated positive returns in every decade since 1880. This isn't backtest curve-fitting; it's a fundamental market feature.

**Key Research:**

| Study | Period | Finding |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Hurst, Ooi & Pedersen (2017) | 1880-2013 | Trend following positive across all asset classes, including during Great Depression |
| Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen (2012) | 1977-2012 | Time-series momentum produces Sharpe ratio of 0.86 across 58 instruments |
| Asness, Moskowitz & Pedersen (2013) | 40+ countries | Momentum pervasive globally, works in equities, bonds, currencies, commodities |

**Crypto-Specific Validation:**

| Strategy | Win Rate | Risk/Reward | Annual Return |
|----------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| 200DMA Trend Following | 40% | 2.5:1 | 45-60% |
| Dual Momentum (12-month) | 35% | 3:1 | 55%+ |
| Turtle System (20-day breakout) | 34% | 2.8:1 | 40%+ |

**The Key Insight:**  
Low win rates (30-40%) are MORE than compensated by asymmetric payoffs. You don't need to be right often—you need to be right BIG when you are.
