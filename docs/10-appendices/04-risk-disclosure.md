## 2. Cryptocurrency Risk Warning

Cryptocurrency investments carry **substantial risk of loss**, including:

### 2.1 Market Risks
- **Extreme Volatility**: Cryptocurrencies can lose 50-90% of value in days or weeks
- **24/7 Trading**: Markets never close; positions can move dramatically overnight
- **Limited Regulation**: Crypto markets lack the protections of traditional financial markets
- **Market Manipulation**: Crypto markets are susceptible to manipulation by large holders
- **Liquidity Risk**: During crises, you may be unable to exit positions at desired prices

### 2.2 Technology Risks
- **Exchange Failure**: Exchanges can be hacked, fail, or freeze withdrawals
- **Smart Contract Bugs**: Code vulnerabilities can lead to total loss
- **Wallet Security**: Private key loss means permanent loss of funds
- **Network Congestion**: Blockchain congestion can prevent transactions
- **Software Bugs**: Trading systems can have bugs causing unintended trades

### 2.3 Regulatory Risks
- **Government Action**: Cryptocurrencies may be banned or restricted in your jurisdiction
- **Tax Changes**: Tax treatment of crypto is uncertain and subject to change
- **Exchange Restrictions**: Exchanges may freeze accounts due to regulatory changes
- **Travel Rule**: International transfers may be blocked or reported

## 4. System Risks

### 4.1 Automation Risks
- **System Failures**: Software bugs, hardware failures, or connectivity issues can cause losses
- **API Failures**: Exchange API downtime can prevent trade execution when needed
- **Data Errors**: Incorrect price data can trigger wrong trades
- **Timing Issues**: Latency can cause slippage and missed opportunities

### 4.2 Strategy Risks
- **Strategy Decay**: Markets evolve; profitable strategies become unprofitable
- **Overfitting**: Backtests may be curve-fit to historical data
- **Crowding**: As more traders use similar strategies, edges disappear
- **Black Swans**: Unpredictable events can cause catastrophic losses

### 4.3 Counterparty Risks
- **Bybit Risk**: Funds held on Bybit are subject to exchange risk
- **Insurance Limitations**: Bybit's insurance fund may not cover all losses
- **ADL Risk**: Auto-deleveraging can close profitable positions
- **Withdrawal Restrictions**: You may not be able to withdraw funds when desired
